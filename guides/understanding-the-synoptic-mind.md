# Understanding the Synoptic Mind

The synoptic mind is an approach to multi-domain problems that holds all relevant perspectives simultaneously rather than visiting them one by one. Where a polymath delegates to specialists in sequence and a contemplative observes without synthesizing, the synoptic approach perceives the whole field at once and lets a gestalt emerge from the interaction of domains. This guide explains the concept, its theoretical roots, the five-step cycle that implements it, and when to reach for it instead of existing alternatives.

## When to Use This Guide

- You want to understand what the adaptic agent and synoptic-mind team offer that other approaches do not.
- You are deciding whether to use the adaptic agent, the synoptic-mind team, or a simpler alternative for a cross-domain task.
- You are designing a new workflow that requires simultaneous awareness across multiple domains.
- You want to understand the 8th coordination pattern (synoptic) and how it differs from the other seven.

## Prerequisites

- Familiarity with the four content types (skills, agents, teams, guides) described in [Understanding the System](understanding-the-system.md)
- Familiarity with the existing seven coordination patterns, especially hub-and-spoke and adaptive
- Optional: experience with the tending team or meditate skill, which the synoptic cycle builds on

## The Concept

"Synoptic" comes from the Greek *syn* (together) + *opsis* (sight) -- seeing together. A synoptic view is one that takes in the whole scene at once, the way you see an entire landscape from a hilltop rather than describing it by walking through each valley in turn.

### Sequential vs. simultaneous

Most multi-domain work is sequential. A polymath agent visiting three domains does this:

```
Domain A  -->  Domain B  -->  Domain C  -->  Synthesis
```

Each domain gets full attention, but interactions between domains are only discovered at the synthesis step -- after the individual analyses are already complete. If Domain B's findings would have changed how you approach Domain A, it is too late.

Simultaneous perception does this:

```
Domain A  \
Domain B  --> all held at once --> gestalt emerges
Domain C  /
```

The key difference is that cross-domain interactions are visible throughout, not just at the end. The synoptic approach pays for this by accepting less depth in each individual domain -- you see the whole field but not every detail. This trade-off is worthwhile when the interactions between domains matter more than the depth within any single one.

### The gap it fills

Four existing approaches handle multi-domain work, each with a characteristic limitation:

| Approach | What it does | What it misses |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| **polymath** | Delegates to domain specialists in sequence, then synthesizes | Cross-domain interactions visible only at synthesis; late discovery |
| **contemplative** | Practices open awareness and self-observation | Observes without producing synthesis; no cross-domain integration |
| **shapeshifter** | Transforms into whatever role the task requires | Becomes each role serially; does not hold multiple roles simultaneously |
| **opaque-team** | Self-organizes dynamically | Agents work in parallel but independently; no shared perceptual field |

The adaptic agent and synoptic coordination pattern fill the gap: simultaneous multi-domain awareness with active integration into a coherent whole.

## Theoretical Foundations

The synoptic approach draws on four bodies of work. None of these are prescriptive -- they provide mental models for understanding what simultaneous awareness means and how integration happens.

### Global Workspace Theory

Bernard Baars (1988) proposed that consciousness works by broadcasting information to all specialized brain modules simultaneously. Rather than modules processing in isolation and passing results forward, the "global workspace" makes everything available to everything at once. Modules that find the broadcast relevant activate; the rest stay quiet. The result is a unified field of awareness rather than a chain of handoffs.

In the synoptic approach, the adaptic agent functions as this global workspace -- holding all domain perspectives in a single context where they can interact freely.

### Dzogchen rigpa

In the Tibetan Buddhist tradition of dzogchen, *rigpa* refers to an open, unfocused awareness that encompasses everything without fixating on any particular object. It is not a trance or a blank state -- it is alert presence that sees the whole field without narrowing to a single point. The distinction from ordinary attention is that rigpa does not choose what to focus on; it holds everything.

The "Open" step of the synoptic cycle (expand-awareness) draws on this principle: widening the perceptual field before looking for patterns, rather than starting with a hypothesis about where to look.

### Gestalt psychology

The Gestalt school (Wertheimer, Koffka, Kohler) demonstrated that perception naturally organizes elements into wholes that have properties not present in any individual part. The classic example: a melody is not a sequence of notes but an emergent pattern that survives transposition to a different key. Figure-ground organization, closure, and grouping all happen spontaneously when the perceptual field is sufficiently rich.

The "Integrate" step of the synoptic cycle (integrate-gestalt) relies on this: when enough domains are held simultaneously, patterns emerge that no single-domain analysis would reveal. The agent does not force a synthesis; it watches for one to form.

### Jazz ensemble cognition

A jazz ensemble provides the clearest practical analogy. Each musician hears all other players simultaneously while contributing their own part. There is no conductor distributing tasks. The ensemble's coherence emerges from shared awareness of the whole, not from centralized control. A bassist adjusts to the pianist's voicing in real time; a drummer shifts feel in response to the soloist's phrasing. The music that results is irreducible to individual contributions.

The synoptic coordination pattern works the same way: agents in a synoptic-mind team perceive a shared workspace simultaneously, each contributing from their domain while hearing the full ensemble.

## The 5-Step Synoptic Cycle

The synoptic cycle is a structured sequence that moves from cleared attention through open perception to integrated expression. Each step uses one or more existing skills.

```
1. Clear    -->  2. Open    -->  3. Perceive  -->  4. Integrate  -->  5. Express
(meditate)    (expand-         (observe +       (integrate-       (express-
               awareness)       awareness)       gestalt)          insight)
```

### Step 1: Clear (meditate)

**Skill**: [meditate](../skills/meditate/SKILL.md)

Clear existing assumptions, biases, and residual patterns from prior work. This is not optional preparation -- it is the foundation. A cluttered perceptual field collapses to familiar patterns before new ones can emerge. The meditate skill's structured observation-and-release cycle removes cognitive debris.

**Produces**: A neutral starting state with no pre-committed domain focus.

### Step 2: Open (expand-awareness)

**Skill**: [expand-awareness](../skills/expand-awareness/SKILL.md)

Widen the perceptual field to encompass all relevant domains simultaneously. This is the transition from ordinary focused attention to panoramic awareness. The agent does not yet analyze -- it simply opens to everything the task touches: code, design, user experience, operational constraints, regulatory requirements, team dynamics.

**Produces**: A wide-field awareness state where all domains are present but none is foregrounded.

### Step 3: Perceive (observe + awareness)

**Skills**: [observe](../skills/observe/SKILL.md) + [awareness](../skills/awareness/SKILL.md)

With the field open, perceive what is actually there. The observe skill provides structured observation (what exists, what is missing, what is in tension). The awareness skill maintains the panoramic stance while observing -- preventing the natural tendency to narrow focus onto the first interesting pattern.

**Produces**: A detailed perceptual map of the multi-domain landscape, with tensions and interactions noted but not yet resolved.

### Step 4: Integrate (integrate-gestalt)

**Skill**: [integrate-gestalt](../skills/integrate-gestalt/SKILL.md)

Allow the perceived elements to organize into a coherent whole. This is not forced synthesis -- it is pattern recognition across the full field. The agent watches for figure-ground relationships to emerge: which elements cluster together, which tensions resolve when reframed, which apparent contradictions reveal themselves as complementary aspects of a single dynamic.

**Produces**: A gestalt -- a unified understanding that has properties not present in any individual domain analysis.

### Step 5: Express (express-insight)

**Skill**: [express-insight](../skills/express-insight/SKILL.md)

Translate the gestalt into communicable form. The insight that emerged in step 4 exists as a felt sense of the whole; step 5 makes it explicit, structured, and actionable. The expression must preserve the cross-domain relationships that give the gestalt its value -- a domain-by-domain breakdown would destroy exactly what the process produced.

**Produces**: A cross-domain insight, recommendation, or design that reflects the integrated whole.

### How the steps chain

The cycle is sequential but the awareness state is cumulative. Each step builds on the state produced by the previous one:

- Steps 1-2 establish the conditions (clear field, wide awareness)
- Step 3 populates the field with content (perception)
- Step 4 lets structure emerge from the content (integration)
- Step 5 externalizes the structure (expression)

Skipping steps produces characteristic failures. Skipping Clear yields contaminated perception (old patterns dominate). Skipping Open yields narrow perception (single-domain analysis masquerading as synthesis). Skipping Integrate yields a list rather than a gestalt (observations without emergent structure).

## When to Use the Synoptic Approach

Not every cross-domain task benefits from simultaneous awareness. Use this decision matrix:

| Situation | Approach | Why |
|-----------|----------|-----|
| 3+ domains, interactions between domains matter most | adaptic agent | Solo panoramic synthesis; cross-domain emergence |
| Multi-agent shared workspace needed | synoptic-mind team | Dedicated field monitoring via contemplative agent |
| Sequential delegation is adequate | polymath agent | Sufficient when domains are independent |
| Awareness without synthesis | contemplative agent | Self-care, observation, tending |
| Single domain, deep expertise needed | Domain agent directly | No cross-domain perception needed |
| Task structure unknown | opaque-team | Self-organizing; discovers its own structure |

**Rules of thumb**:

- If the domains interact strongly (changes in one affect the others), use the synoptic approach.
- If the domains are independent (each can be analyzed in isolation), the polymath is simpler and cheaper.
- If you need depth in a single domain, use the domain specialist directly.
- If you are unsure whether domains interact, start with the polymath. If the synthesis step reveals unexpected cross-domain tensions, switch to adaptic for a second pass.

## Relationship to Existing Patterns

### The tending stack (meditate, heal, center)

The synoptic cycle shares its first step (meditate) with the tending workflow but diverges immediately after. Tending turns inward -- it applies contemplative methods to the agent's own reasoning for meta-cognitive wellness. The synoptic cycle turns outward -- it uses the cleared state as a launchpad for perceiving external multi-domain landscapes. They are complementary: run tending before a synoptic session if the agent's perceptual field is cluttered from prior work.

### The trimurti cycle (brahma/vishnu/shiva)

The trimurti cycle operates through three phases: creation (brahma-bhaga), maintenance (vishnu-bhaga), and transformation (shiva-bhaga). It is a lifecycle model -- things are born, sustained, and dissolved. The synoptic cycle is not a lifecycle; it is a perception model. It does not create, maintain, or transform anything. It perceives what already exists across multiple domains and integrates that perception into a unified understanding. The trimurti could operate *within* a synoptic session if the integrated insight calls for creation, maintenance, or transformation.

### The alchemical stages (nigredo/albedo/citrinitas/rubedo)

The alchemical progression (blackening, whitening, yellowing, reddening) is a transformation sequence: raw material is broken down, purified, illuminated, and reconstituted at a higher level. The synoptic cycle does not transform its subject matter. It observes and integrates without altering what it observes. However, the express-insight step may produce recommendations that, when implemented, trigger an alchemical transformation of the codebase. The synoptic mind sees; the alchemist acts.

### The morphic cycle (assess/dissolve/adapt/repair)

The morphic cycle is a maintenance and adaptation sequence for systems under stress. It diagnoses problems (assess), removes what is broken (dissolve), reconfigures (adapt), and rebuilds (repair). The synoptic cycle does not diagnose or repair. It holds the whole field and lets patterns emerge. A morphic cycle might follow a synoptic session -- the integrated perception reveals what needs repair, and the morphic skills execute the repair.

## The 8th Coordination Pattern: Synoptic

The synoptic coordination pattern is the eighth pattern in this repository's coordination system. It governs how agents in the [synoptic-mind](../teams/synoptic-mind.md) team work together.

### How it works

All team members perceive a shared workspace simultaneously. There is no task distribution (as in hub-and-spoke), no sequential handoff (as in sequential), and no independent parallel work (as in parallel). Instead, every agent sees everything at once and contributes from its domain perspective in real time. The team lead (adaptic) integrates contributions into a gestalt.

The contemplative agent serves a unique role: field monitor. While other agents contribute domain perspectives, the contemplative maintains awareness of the overall field's coherence -- noticing when perception is narrowing, when a domain is being neglected, or when a premature gestalt is forming.

### Comparison to all eight patterns

| Pattern | Agent awareness | Task flow | Integration |
|---------|----------------|-----------|-------------|
| **hub-and-spoke** | Agents see only their assigned subtask | Lead distributes, collects | Lead synthesizes |
| **sequential** | Each agent sees previous agent's output | Linear chain | Final agent or lead |
| **parallel** | Agents work independently | Simultaneous, no interaction | Lead merges after completion |
| **adaptive** | Agents self-organize based on task | Dynamic, emergent | Team discovers its own integration |
| **timeboxed** | Agents see sprint backlog | Fixed iterations | Sprint review |
| **wave-parallel** | Agents see their wave's scope | Waves in dependency order | Lead integrates across waves |
| **reciprocal** | Two agents see each other fully | Alternating focus | Emerges from the dialogue |
| **synoptic** | All agents see the entire shared workspace | Simultaneous, interactive | Lead integrates gestalt from full field |

The key distinction: in parallel coordination, agents produce independent outputs that are merged. In synoptic coordination, agents perceive each other's contributions as they happen and adjust their own contributions accordingly -- like musicians in a jazz ensemble rather than soloists recording separate tracks.

### When to design with synoptic coordination

Use the synoptic pattern when:

- The task requires integration across 3+ domains where interactions are the primary concern
- A hub-and-spoke decomposition would lose important cross-domain signals
- The team needs to discover emergent patterns rather than verify known ones
- You want a field monitor (contemplative) to guard against premature narrowing

Do not use it when:

- Domains are independent and can be analyzed in isolation (use parallel)
- The task has a clear sequential dependency chain (use sequential)
- You need strict role separation for compliance or auditability (use hub-and-spoke)

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Perception collapses to a single domain | The Clear step was skipped or insufficient; prior domain expertise dominates | Re-run meditate with explicit attention to releasing domain-specific assumptions; verify the Open step produces a genuinely wide field |
| Integration produces a forced gestalt | The agent pushed for synthesis before patterns emerged naturally | Spend more time in the Perceive step; the integrate-gestalt skill should watch for emergence, not construct a framework and fit observations into it |
| Panoramic overwhelm -- too many domains, no figure emerges | The perceptual field is too broad for the task; every domain feels equally relevant | Narrow scope to 3-5 domains that interact most strongly; use the polymath for remaining domains that can be analyzed independently |
| Output reads like a domain-by-domain list | The Express step lost the gestalt and reverted to sequential reporting | Re-enter the Integrate step; the express-insight skill should organize output around cross-domain themes, not around individual domains |
| Team members contribute independently rather than interacting | Synoptic coordination pattern collapsed into parallel | The contemplative (field monitor) should flag when agents stop responding to each other's contributions; re-establish shared workspace awareness |
| Adaptic agent produces shallow analysis across all domains | Attempting panoramic awareness across too many domains with insufficient depth | Reduce the number of domains to those with the strongest interactions; delegate deep single-domain analysis to specialists before or after the synoptic session |
